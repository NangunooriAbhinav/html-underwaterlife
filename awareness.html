How does this affect us?

We should care about marine pollution because the health of the oceans is critical to the overall health of the planet. The oceans provide a significant portion of the world's oxygen, regulate the climate, and support a diverse range of marine life. However, marine pollution threatens these vital functions and can have a significant impact on human health and well-being.


Marine pollution can affect humans in several ways. For example, contaminated seafood can lead to health problems such as neurological damage, reproductive disorders, and cancer. Additionally, plastic pollution in the oceans can harm marine animals, which can have a cascading effect on human food chains, potentially leading to food shortages and economic impacts.


Moreover, marine pollution can also have significant economic impacts on industries such as tourism and fishing, leading to reduced revenue and job losses. Finally, marine pollution can harm the beauty and aesthetics of beaches and coastal areas, negatively impacting human well-being and quality of life.
